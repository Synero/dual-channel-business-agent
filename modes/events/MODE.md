# Mode: Events

**For:** Businesses that organize experiences, tours, classes, or events with limited capacity.

## Examples
- City tours, food tours, adventure tours
- Cooking classes, art workshops, dance classes
- Yoga retreats, meditation sessions
- Corporate events, team building
- Concerts, pop-ups, markets
- Private dining experiences, wine tastings

## Customer Intent
The customer wants to:
1. Know what events/tours are coming up
2. Check availability (dates, spots left)
3. Book spots for themselves or a group
4. Know what's included, duration, meeting point

## Conversation Flow
```
Customer: "Hola, tienen tours este finde?"
→ Greet briefly, list upcoming events with dates and spots left

Customer: "Quiero el tour gastronómico del sábado"
→ Confirm event, check spots available, ask for group size

Customer: "Somos 4 personas"
→ Confirm 4 spots, calculate total, ask for names

Customer: "Dale, los 4"
→ Confirm booking, send payment details or say "pay at arrival"

Customer: "Transferimos"
→ Confirm payment, send meeting point details, remind what to bring
```

## Key Data
- **Event Catalog:** Upcoming events with dates, capacity, price, description
- **Bookings:** Who booked, how many spots, payment status
- **Waitlist:** People interested if spots open up
- **Customer Memory:** Past events attended, dietary restrictions, group size preference

## Payment Patterns
- Full payment to secure spot (most common)
- Deposit to reserve, balance before event
- Pay on arrival (for low-demand events)
- Group discounts

## What Makes This Mode Unique
- **Capacity is hard limit** — once full, no more sales
- **Date-specific** — event happens once, then it's gone
- **Experience matters** — customer asks "what's included?"
- **Group dynamics** — often booking for multiple people
- **Reminders important** — no-shows waste spots and money
