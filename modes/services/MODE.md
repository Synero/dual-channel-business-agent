# Mode: Services

**For:** Businesses that sell time slots and appointments, not physical products.

## Examples
- Hair salons, barbershops, nail salons
- Massage therapists, spas, physical therapy
- Dentists, veterinarians, medical clinics
- Mechanics, car washes, repair services
- Private tutors, fitness coaches, yoga classes
- Pet groomers, cleaning services, plumbing

## Customer Intent
The customer wants to:
1. Know what services are offered and how much they cost
2. Book an appointment for a specific date/time
3. Reschedule or cancel an existing appointment
4. Know location/address and how to get there

## Conversation Flow
```
Customer: "Hola, tienen horas para corte de pelo?"
→ Greet briefly, list services with prices and typical duration

Customer: "Quiero agendar un corte para mañana"
→ Ask preferred time, check availability, confirm slot

Customer: "A las 15:00 me sirve"
→ Confirm appointment, ask for name, give address/location

Customer: "Necesito cambiar a las 17:00"
→ Check new slot, confirm reschedule, update calendar

Customer: "Cancelo porfa"
→ Confirm cancellation politely, free the slot
```

## Key Data
- **Service Catalog:** Services offered, prices, duration, description
- **Availability Calendar:** Open slots per day (updated in real-time)
- **Appointments:** Booked slots with customer info, service, status
- **Customer Memory:** Preferred stylist/professional, usual time, service history

## Payment Patterns
- Pay on arrival (most common)
- Pre-pay deposit to secure slot (high-demand services)
- Package deals ("10 sessions for X price")
- Membership/recurring (monthly unlimited)

## What Makes This Mode Unique
- **Time is the product** — once a slot passes, it's gone forever
- **No-shows hurt** — confirmation reminders are critical
- **Professional relationship** — customer often has a preferred provider
- **Location matters** — customer needs to physically arrive
